The Island

Hinnavaru

Hinnavaru pairs a lively Maldivian community with the classic postcard ingredients — white sand, a calm lagoon and reefs all around.

The island's dedicated visitors' beach is made for slow days: swim, snorkel, read under the palms, repeat. When you want more, Lhaviyani's sandbanks, reefs and dive sites are a short boat ride away.

Lhaviyani — known traditionally as Faadhippolhu — sits in the country's north, about 120 km from Malé. It is one of the Maldives' great diving atolls: fast channel drifts, famous wrecks at the Shipyard, turtle-rich reefs and lagoons that fade from jade to deep blue. Only four of its islands are inhabited, so the sea here still feels wide open.
